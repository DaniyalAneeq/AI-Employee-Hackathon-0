**Topic:** AI Employee

## Post Content

I built myself an employee who never sleeps, never takes a sick day, and works for the cost of a coffee subscription.

It reads my emails. Drafts replies. Monitors LinkedIn. Schedules posts. And every morning it hands me a briefing with everything I need to know before I open a single app.

I call it my AI Employee.

Here is what surprised me most: the value is not in the automation itself. It is in getting my attention back.

Every hour I spent triaging emails or deciding what to post on LinkedIn was an hour I was not working on the things only I can do. Strategy. Client relationships. Creative thinking.

The AI Employee handles the inbox. I handle the decisions that actually matter.

This is not science fiction. I built the whole system using Claude Code, Python, Playwright, and an Obsidian vault as the brain. Every action the AI wants to take goes through a human approval step first. Nothing gets sent without my say so.

The future of work for solo founders and small teams is not hiring faster. It is building smarter systems that multiply what you can do with the time you already have.

Are you building AI systems into your workflow yet? What is the first task you would hand off?
